Welcome to ArttulOS

ArttulOS is an approachable and user-friendly distribution based on the powerful Arch Linux core. Our mission is to bridge the gap between the power and flexibility of Arch and the simplicity that everyday computer users expect. We aim to make the Arch ecosystem accessible to a wider audience—from students and developers to casual desktop users—by providing a stable, pre-configured, and beautiful desktop experience right out of the box.

This project is made possible by the incredible work of the Arch Linux developers and the broader open-source community. We see ourselves as curators, taking an already fantastic base and adding a layer of convenience, rigorous testing, and thoughtful design to create a cohesive and welcoming operating system for everyone.

Our Philosophy & How You Can Help

We Are Open, Independent, and Bootstrapped

Everything related to ArttulOS, including this website, is fully open-source. We believe in the power of free and open-source software (FOSS) to empower users and protect digital freedom. We are a small, passionate team of developers, and our independence is not just a feature—it's the foundation of our project. We are completely self-funded and self-hosted, ensuring that our decisions are driven by our community, not by corporate interests.

Our unique funding model is designed to protect this independence. We are developing separate, proprietary applications for other operating systems (like Windows and macOS). The revenue from these projects directly funds the infrastructure and development of ArttulOS, allowing our Linux-based work to remain free, open, and independent forever. This is our way of contributing to the open-source world while building a sustainable future for our project.

Under the Hood

ArttulOS is more than just a themed version of Arch Linux. We've made several key decisions and customizations to improve the user experience, stability, and performance.

Boot Process: Simple & Reliable

We use a combination of bootloaders to ensure a smooth experience. The live environment is booted using systemd-boot and syslinux for maximum hardware compatibility. For the final installation, we use grub, a powerful and widely-supported bootloader. This hybrid approach ensures that ArttulOS boots reliably on a wide range of hardware, including both modern UEFI systems and older BIOS-based machines. Don't worry about the technical details - it just works.

Filesystem: EXT4 for Stability

We use the EXT4 filesystem by default. While newer filesystems exist, EXT4 is the most mature, stable, and well-tested filesystem in the Linux world. It offers excellent performance for desktop use cases and is incredibly reliable, ensuring your data is safe. This choice reflects our commitment to providing a stable and worry-free experience.

Audio: The Power of Pipewire

ArttulOS uses Pipewire as its audio server, a modern replacement for PulseAudio. Pipewire is superior in every way: it offers lower latency, better CPU utilization, and seamless support for both consumer and professional audio applications. It also unifies video and audio streams, providing a more robust multimedia experience.

Using Your Own Flashing Tool

After downloading an ISO, you will need to write it to a USB drive (8GB or larger recommended). This will erase all data on the USB drive.

Balena Etcher (Recommended for most users)

Balena Etcher is a simple graphical tool for Windows, macOS, and Linux. 1. Select the downloaded ArttulOS ISO file. 2. Select your USB drive. 3. Click "Flash!".

Ventoy (for multi-boot USBs)

Ventoy is a tool that lets you create a bootable USB drive where you can simply copy multiple ISO files. 1. Install Ventoy to your USB drive once. 2. Drag and drop the ArttulOS ISO file onto the USB. 3. Boot from the USB to see the Ventoy menu.

Using `dd` (Advanced Linux Users)

The `dd` command is a powerful but dangerous tool. Incorrect usage can lead to data loss. Be certain of your USB drive's device name (e.g., `/dev/sdb`, not `/dev/sdb1`).

sudo dd if=/path/to/ArttulOS.iso of=/dev/sdX bs=4M status=progress oflag=sync

Replace `/path/to/ArttulOS.iso` with the actual path to your downloaded file and `/dev/sdX` with your USB device identifier.

Why We Don't Take Your Money

We've had people ask, but we haven't taken any donations. Why? To put it simply, we are developers, not accountants. Accepting donations would require us to register as a non-profit organization, which involves significant legal and administrative overhead. This includes incorporation fees, yearly compliance paperwork, and complex tax filings. These tasks would divert our limited time and resources away from what we do best: building a great Linux distribution.

More importantly, it would change the nature of our project. We want to remain a community-driven project, where decisions are made based on what's best for the users, not on financial incentives. Becoming a corporate entity, even a non-profit one, would introduce a level of bureaucracy that could stifle innovation and distract us from our core mission.

So, How Can You Help?

Instead of donating money, you can help us by reaching out to potential sponsors for open-source projects. We need resources like mirrors, hardware for testing, and other infrastructure to grow. By helping us build relationships with these sponsors, you can make a much bigger impact than a monetary donation.

If you have connections to companies that sponsor open-source projects, please get in touch with us. Your help in this area will allow us to grow sustainably and stay true to our community-focused mission.
